The Changing Face of Battleground Districts

Date: <Enter Date Here>

2016 gave the US population a look into how a politician can activate a particular group of people to achieve victory, even when victory seems out of reach.

As the face of America changes, politicians in battleground districts will have to more closely examine their constituents and cater to the policies they care most about.

As of 2018 there are 80 battleground districts

Within these districts, foreign born Americans are growing quickly, and are increasing in their proportion of the electorate. In 2018 this wonâ€™t likely be the difference between winning and losing, as there is only one district where the growth of the foreign born electorate exceeds the 2016 margin of victory.

However, that fact is going to quickly change in the coming elections.

By 2020, 6 districts will have a new foreign born electorate population that exceeds the 2016 margin of victory. By 2022, there will be an estimated 15 districts, and by 2024, 18 districts, with many more following in the years after.

This may not seem like a lot of districts.

However, keep in mind that a 20 district swing for one party over the other, could be the difference between controlling the house or not. In our era of politics where a thin margin separates Republican and Democrat controlled congresses, any advantage one party can gain over the other is vital for success.

Even greater change can be seen when you compare the districts by population size.

When you remove the variable of land size in favor of a closer look at the influence over the entire population, you can begin to see that foreign born electorates are going to be a strong voice throughout America. Those nearly 20 districts account for approximately 14 million Americans, which is 5+ times the margin of victory in the popular vote between Donald Trump and Hillary Clinton.
